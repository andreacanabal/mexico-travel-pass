🌮 THE MEXICO FOOD RULES
APPLIES IN ALL 3 CITIES
These rules apply in Mexico City, Guadalajara, and Monterrey. Learn them once. Use them everywhere.
01
WATER
BOTTLED WATER ONLY. ALWAYS. EVERYWHERE.
Tap water is not potable in any Mexican city. Brush teeth only. Never drink it.

Budget 3–4L per day. ~$10–15 MXN per 1.5L bottle.
Ice at sit-down restaurants = safe (commercial purified ice).
Ice at street stalls = skip.
02
THE TURNOVER TEST
Long line of locals = fresh food = safe.
Empty stall at peak hours = food sitting = skip.

This single rule eliminates 80% of food risk.
03
HEAT = SAFE
Steaming hot or thoroughly cold = safe.
Lukewarm = bacteria zone. Skip it.

Works for all foods, all cities.
04
BUILD UP SLOWLY
Day 1–2: Stick to well-cooked hot foods.
Day 3+: Your gut bacteria are adjusting. Expand.
Day 5+: You can eat most things locals eat.

Don't eat everything on Day 1 just because you can.
05
ALCOHOL DOUBLES RISK
Alcohol reduces stomach acid that kills bacteria.
Drinking + risky street food = high chance of food poisoning.

Eat a full meal before drinking. Drink water between drinks. Especially in Monterrey heat.
06
RAW VEGETABLES = CAUTION
Washed in what water? At sit-down restaurants with purified water: fine.

Raw garnishes at street stalls (onion, cilantro, radish):
Skip on Days 1–2. Your call by Day 3.
07
SUERO ORAL IS YOUR BEST FRIEND
Buy a pack of Suero Oral sachets at your first pharmacy visit. ~$30–50 MXN.

Before going to any stadium: drink one.
After heavy drinking: drink one before bed.
If you feel stomach issues starting: drink one.

This single product prevents 90% of serious cases.

✅ GREEN — Safe everywhere

✅Tacos from busy taquerías (hot protein, fresh tortilla)
✅Soups and caldos (boiling liquid)
✅Tamales (steamed and sealed)
✅Freshly grilled meats (asada, pastor from spinning spit)
✅Food from restaurants with sit-down service
✅Commercial bottled water and drinks (Coca-Cola, etc.)
✅Churros and fried sweets (high heat cooking)

⚠️ YELLOW — Use caution

⚠️Raw salsas (Days 1–2: skip or minimal)
⚠️Fresh juices (restaurant = fine, street barrel = ask)
⚠️Raw garnishes (onion, cilantro, radish)
⚠️Seafood away from coasts (inland cities = extra caution)
⚠️Ice from unidentified source (restaurant = fine)

🔴 RED — Avoid everywhere

🔴Tap water in any form (drinking or tap ice cubes)
🔴Food sitting in sun at 34°C+ (Monterrey especially)
🔴Seafood at street stalls in landlocked cities
🔴Anything lukewarm that should be hot or cold
🔴Raw meat preparations

MEXICO CITY 🏙️
⚡ KEY RISK: ALTITUDE + RAW SALSAS
⛰️ ALTITUDE + STOMACH
2,240 meters slows your digestion.
Eat smaller portions for first 2 days.
Avoid heavy late-night meals first 3 days.
Dehydration at altitude is 30% faster than at sea level.

CDMX Signature Dishes

✅Tacos al pastor — Classic, spinning spit, very safe
✅Tlayudas — Flat, fully cooked, safe
✅Chilaquiles — Cooked tortillas + cooked sauce
✅Quesadillas — Made fresh on comal (ask 'con queso')
⚠️Raw street salsas — Skip Days 1–2
Best areas: Roma Norte, Condesa, Polanco
Pharmacy: Farmacias del Ahorro (red signage, 200+ locations)
Hospital: ABC — +52 55 5230-8000
GUADALAJARA 🌮
⚡ KEY RISK: SPICY SAUCES + TEQUILA
🍴 GDL FOOD CAPITAL
Guadalajara is the food capital of Mexico. Birria, torta ahogada, pozole, tejuino.
The risks here are not the iconic dishes — they are serving temp and your spice tolerance.

GDL Signature Dishes

✅Birria with consomé — Hot, cooked, high turnover
✅Torta ahogada — Ask 'enchilada' (mild) on Day 1
✅Pozole — Boiling hot soup
⚠️Tejuino — From refrigerated vendors only
⚠️Tequila — Eat full meal first, water between shots
🌶️ Torta ahogada full spice on Day 1 of your trip is a common tourist mistake. Go mild first.
Best areas: Chapultepec corridor, Tlaquepaque
Pharmacy: Farmacias del Ahorro / Farmacias Guadalajara
Hospital: Country 2000 — +52 33 3648-8585

⛰️ CDMX ALTITUDE NOTE
Recovery takes slightly longer at 2,240m. Your body is working harder. Add 6–12 hours to expected recovery time.
🌶️ GDL: SPICE CRASH VS FOOD POISONING
Burning stomach from torta ahogada resolves in 2–6 hours with milk/bread/rest. Food poisoning takes 24–48 hours. If it's getting worse after 4 hours: food poisoning.
🔥 MTY: HEAT + FOOD SICKNESS
Cool down first, then treat stomach. If you're in 34°C sun while sick: get to air conditioning immediately. Then hydrate.
