CDMX STOMACH GUIDE
Food Safety
WHAT TO EAT IN MEXICO CITY
20–50% of travelers get sick. These are the rules that keep you eating everything.
💡
The #1 rule: if locals are lined up for it — it's safe. Empty stall = skip it.
🟢 EAT FREELY
These are safe — hot, fresh, high turnover
🌮 Tacos al Pastor
✅ Safe at any busy taquería
▼
Why safe
Protein cooked on a rotating vertical spit at high heat continuously. One of the safest street foods in Mexico City.
How to order
"Tres tacos de pastor, por favor"
Watch for
See the trompo (spit) spinning yourself. If there's no visible spit — it's not real al pastor.
🧺 Tacos de Canasta
✅ Safe — a CDMX morning tradition
▼
Why safe
Steamed in baskets, kept hot. Very high turnover. Look for the cyclist with the basket bike.
Best spots
Roma Norte and Condesa markets, mornings only.
🫔 Tamales
✅ Safe — steamed, sealed, hot
▼
Why safe
Cooked inside corn husk = protected from contamination. Very safe street food.
When to get them
Mornings only — sold from pots of steam.
🧀 Quesadillas (cooked to order)
✅ Safe when made fresh in front of you
▼
Why safe
Cooked on comal directly in front of you at high heat. Ask for 'con queso' — melted cheese = hot.
CDMX pro tip 🔥
In CDMX, quesadillas don't automatically come with cheese. Say "con queso" or you won't get it. Yes, this is an ongoing national argument.
🍲 Soups / Caldos
✅ Extremely safe — boiling hot liquid
▼
Examples
Caldo de pollo, pozole, menudo
Note
Menudo (tripe soup) is the legendary hangover cure. Don't be scared of what it is.
🍽️ Enchiladas / Chilaquiles
✅ Safe at any sit-down restaurant
▼
Why safe
Cooked sauce, cooked tortilla, cooked protein. Very low risk food.
🥤 Agua de Horchata / Jamaica
✅ Safe at established places
▼
Caveat
Only from restaurants using purified water. Not from open barrels at street stalls without ice.
🟡 USE CAUTION
Read before eating these
🌶️ Street Salsas
⚠️ First 2 days — go easy
▼
Why risky
Raw chiles, raw onion, sometimes unfiltered water in prep. Your gut needs time to adjust. After Day 3, you're more adapted.
Safe approach
Ask for cooked salsa (salsa cocida) instead of raw (salsa cruda) your first 2 days.
🥗 Raw Salads at Street Stalls
⚠️ Skip at stalls, fine at restaurants
▼
Why risky
Washed in what water? At sit-down restaurants with filtered water — fine. Street stall raw veg — higher risk.
🍹 Fresh Juices
⚠️ Only from places using filtered water
▼
How to check
Ask: "¿Usa agua purificada?" (Do you use purified water?) Any serious juice bar says yes immediately.
🦐 Ceviche
⚠️ Restaurant only, not street stalls
▼
Why
CDMX is not a coastal city. Seafood travels. At a good restaurant with high turnover = fine. At a random street stall = risky.
🧊 Ice
⚠️ Restaurants = safe, random stalls = skip
▼
Rule
Restaurants use commercial ice from purified water. Say "sin hielo" (without ice) only if you're unsure about the source.
🔴 SKIP THESE
Not worth the risk
🦞 Seafood from Street Stalls
❌ Avoid
▼
CDMX is 400km from the ocean. Street-stall mariscos have a supply chain problem. Eat seafood at proper marisquerías only.
☀️ Food Sitting in Sun
❌ Avoid if not steaming or refrigerated
▼
The danger zone is 5°C–60°C — exactly where street food sits on a hot Mexico City afternoon. If it's not steaming hot or visibly refrigerated: skip it.
🧈 Unrefrigerated Dairy
❌ Avoid at street stalls in heat
▼
Crema, cheese, sour cream sitting open in sun. Fine inside restaurants. Street stall crema in 30°C heat = skip it or eat immediately.
🚰 Tap Water — EVER
❌ Non-negotiable. Never drink it.
CDMX tap water is not potable. Brush your teeth with it only. Drinking = guaranteed stomach problems within 24 hours.
⛰️ Your Stomach at 2,240m (7,350ft)
Digestion slows at altitude. You may feel full faster, experience more bloating, or feel nauseous even from food that would normally be fine. This is normal.

Eat smaller portions your first 2 days. Avoid heavy meals late at night for the first 3 days.
FARMACIAS DEL AHORRO
The red pharmacy chain. 200+ locations in CDMX. Many open 24hrs. Doctor on duty ~$50–100 MXN.
🔍 How to Find One
Search "Farmacias del Ahorro" on Google Maps — red signage, everywhere.

Farmacias Similares — Dr. Simi mascot, cheapest prices.

SUERO ORAL / PEDIALYTE
Oral Rehydration Salts
Spanish ask: "Suero oral, por favor"
Replaces electrolytes and fluids lost from diarrhea or vomiting. MORE IMPORTANT than any medication. Take this first before anything else.
Also: ELECTROLIT — best electrolyte drink in Mexico. Sold at OXXO, pharmacies, everywhere.
Dose: Drink one full packet dissolved in water. Repeat every time you have diarrhea.
⚠️ Dehydration at altitude is faster and more dangerous than at sea level.
~$30–50 MXN Electrolit ~$25 MXN
LOPERAMIDA
Imodium (loperamide) · Stops diarrhea
Spanish ask: "Loperamida, por favor"
Slows intestinal movement. Stops diarrhea. Brands: Imodium, Lopex, Lomotil.
Dose: 2mg initial dose, then 2mg after each loose stool. Maximum 8mg per day.
⚠️ Do NOT take if you have fever or blood in stool — see a doctor instead. Does not cure infection — just stops symptoms. Always combine with Suero Oral.
~$25–40 MXN
METOCLOPRAMIDA
Reglan (metoclopramide) · Stops nausea
Spanish ask: "Metoclopramida para náuseas"
Stops nausea and vomiting.
Alternative: Dramamine (dimenhydrinato) — widely available, also helps with nausea.
~$30–50 MXN
PARACETAMOL / IBUPROFENO
Acetaminophen / Ibuprofen · Pain & Fever
Spanish ask: "Paracetamol para el dolor"
For stomach issues, paracetamol is gentler than ibuprofen. Avoid ibuprofen on an empty stomach.
~$20–35 MXN
FLORASTOR / LACTEOL FORT
Probiotic · Restores gut bacteria
Spanish ask: "Probiótico para diarrea del viajero"
Take during and after a stomach episode. Also good to take before you suspect risky food.
~$80–150 MXN
OMEPRAZOL / PEPTO-BISMOL
Antacid · Heartburn & indigestion
Spanish ask: "Omeprazol" or "Pepto Bismol"
Reduces stomach acid. Good for heartburn and indigestion from spicy food. Pepto Bismol has mild antibacterial properties too.
~$30–60 MXN

🏥 When to See a Doctor — Not the Pharmacy
  • ❌ Fever over 38.5°C (101.3°F)
  • ❌ Blood in stool
  • ❌ Symptoms lasting more than 48 hours
  • ❌ You cannot keep any fluids down
  • ❌ Extreme thirst, no urination for 8+ hours, dizziness on standing

    YOU'RE SICK.
    Step by step. In order. Starting now.
    1
    💧 HYDRATE FIRST (most important)
    Before ANY medication: drink Suero Oral or Electrolit. Half a liter right now. Another half liter in 1 hour. This is more important than Imodium. Dehydration at 2,240m altitude hits faster.
    2
    🚫 Stop Eating Solid Food
    For 4–6 hours: nothing solid. Clear liquids only: water, electrolytes, clear broth. Your gut needs to rest.
    3
    💊 Take Medication
    Once you've hydrated:
    • Loperamida 2mg — stop diarrhea
    • Metoclopramida — if nauseous
    • Paracetamol — if fever or pain
    4
    🍌 BRAT Diet (when ready to eat)
    After 6–8 hours: Bananas · Rice · Applesauce · Toast
    In Mexico: plátano + arroz blanco + pan tostado. Low fiber, easy to digest.
    5
    😴 Rest — Seriously
    You need to sleep. This is not optional. A World Cup match is not worth a hospital visit. Sleep 8 hours: you'll feel 80% better.
    6
    🍜 Reintroduce Food Slowly
    After 12 hours feeling better: start with soup (caldo), crackers, banana.

    Avoid for 24 more hours: spicy food, alcohol, dairy, raw vegetables.
    ❌ What NOT to Do
  • ❌ Don't "push through" and go to the match if you have a fever
  • ❌ Don't drink alcohol — dehydrates you more
  • ❌ Don't take antibiotics without a doctor
  • ❌ Don't eat spicy food to "reset" your stomach
  • ❌ Don't ignore blood in stool or high fever
    • ⏱️ The 48-Hour Test
    If you are NOT improving after 48 hours: you need a doctor. This could be bacterial, not viral.
