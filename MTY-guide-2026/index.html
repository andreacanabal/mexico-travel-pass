Heat Warning — Read This First
Monterrey in June hits 34°C (93°F) with high humidity. Under direct sun it feels like 42°C (107°F). Heat exhaustion is a real risk. Tap to read your survival protocol.
The Day Before: Drink 3L water. Avoid alcohol — it dehydrates you.

Match Morning: Eat a full meal. Apply SPF 50+. Start drinking water at breakfast. Don't wait for thirst — thirst means you're already dehydrated.

At the Stadium: Buy water at every opportunity. Sit in shade if possible. If dizzy or you stop sweating — find medical staff immediately.

Signs of heat exhaustion: heavy sweating, weakness, cold/pale skin, fast weak pulse, nausea.
Heat stroke (EMERGENCY): hot, red, dry skin + confusion + fast strong pulse → Call 911 immediately.

Buy at Farmacias del Ahorro: Electrolit ($30 MXN), Suero Oral/Pedialyte ($30-50 MXN), SPF 50 ($80-150 MXN). Say: "Me siento mal por el calor"

02 SAFETY & NEIGHBORHOODS
HEAT SURVIVAL — MATCH DAY PROTOCOL
PHASE 1 — THE DAY BEFORE
Hydrate aggressively the day before. You cannot catch up once you're dehydrated on match day. Drink 3L of water the day before a daytime match. Avoid alcohol the night before — it dehydrates you.
PHASE 2 — MATCH MORNING
Eat a real meal — your body needs fuel to regulate heat. Apply SPF 50+ sunscreen. Reapply every 2 hours. Buy a hat if you don't have one (stadium shops sell them). Start drinking water at breakfast. Don't wait for thirst — thirst means you're already dehydrated.
PHASE 3 — AT THE STADIUM
Buy water at every opportunity inside the stadium. Sit in shade if your seat allows. If you feel dizzy or stop sweating: that is a medical risk.

Find stadium medical staff immediately — don't wait.

Signs of heat exhaustion: heavy sweating, weakness, cold/pale/clammy skin, fast/weak pulse, nausea.
HEAT STROKE (EMERGENCY): hot/red/dry skin, fast/strong pulse, confusion → Call 911 immediately.

NEIGHBORHOOD SAFETY ZONES
Safe  ·  Caution  ·  Avoid — Tap to expand
San Pedro Garza García
Safest municipality in Latin America
▶
The Beverly Hills of Latin America. Lowest crime index in the entire Monterrey metro (20.5/100). Private security patrols, luxury hotels, upscale restaurants. Calzada del Valle and Centrito Valle are the main strips. Jazz bars, rooftop terraces, international cuisine. Safe day and night including walking alone.
Getting there: Uber from anywhere ~15–25 min.
Barrio Antiguo
Historic quarter, best nightlife
▶
Monterrey's historic quarter. Cobblestone streets, colonial architecture, best nightlife in the city. Padre Mier is the main street — clubs, bars, live music. Safe with company. Don't wander empty side streets alone after 2am. Stay on the main strip.
Getting there: Metro Line 1 → Padre Mier station or Uber.
Macroplaza / Centro
Gran Plaza, Faro del Comercio
▶
The Gran Plaza is one of the largest public plazas in the world. Government buildings, Faro del Comercio, Paseo Santa Lucía canal. Beautiful and safe by day. Fine on main streets at night. Avoid empty side streets after 10pm. Not a nightlife destination — use for tourism and transit.
Paseo Santa Lucía
3.5km riverside promenade
▶
3.5km riverside promenade connecting Macroplaza to Parque Fundidora. Well-lit, popular, families and tourists. Very safe even at night. Outdoor restaurants, boats, public art. One of the best urban walks in Mexico.
Valle Oriente
Modern business district
▶
Modern business district with international hotels, upscale malls (Galerías Valle Oriente), restaurants. Safe, clean, very tourist-friendly. Good base for fans who want maximum safety and minimum adventure.
Guadalupe / Stadium Area
Estadio BBVA zone
▶
Estadio BBVA is here. The stadium itself is safe. The main roads around it are fine for match days with the massive security presence. Do NOT explore residential areas around the stadium on foot. Take your designated exit route and leave. Avoid lingering after matches in unlit areas.
Independencia
▶
Do not visit. Highest crime area in Monterrey. No tourist reason to go here.
Niño Artillero
▶
Avoid entirely. Flagged by security analysts as a high-risk zone for tourists.
Solidaridad
▶
Cartel-related activity reported. Not a tourist area.

The US Embassy lifted ALL movement restrictions for Monterrey on February 24, 2026. Nuevo León has a Level 3 state advisory but this reflects rural southern areas — not the tourist circuit.

Estadio BBVA is in Guadalupe — east of city center. There is NO direct metro stop at the stadium. Best route: Metro Line 1 → Exposición → 10–12 minute walk via pedestrian bridge. Alternatively: Uber, but expect severe match-day delays.

MTY Airport Uber: Uber is FULLY LEGAL at Monterrey Airport. Unlike Cancún, Cabo, and Puerto Vallarta where Uber has restrictions — MTY has zero restrictions. Request directly from arrivals.
ARRIVING AT MTY AIRPORT
MTY has 3 terminals (A, B, C) — all connected by underground walkway and free shuttle. Most international flights: Terminal B. On arrival: follow signs to ground transport.
Same Rule as Every Mexican Airport
Do NOT accept rides from people approaching you in the arrivals hall. Use Uber app or the official taxi kiosk inside the terminal only.
Uber — Fully legal at MTY. Pickup area clearly marked outside Terminal B exit.
Official Taxi — Taxi Aeropuerto kiosks inside terminal. Fixed rate by zone. Pay in advance, show voucher to driver.

To downtown: $250–350 MXN · ~25–40 min
To San Pedro: $200–280 MXN · ~20–30 min

AFTER THE FINAL WHISTLE
Post-Match Crowd Warning
53,000 people leaving at once. The area around the stadium entrance is extremely crowded for 30–40 minutes post-match. Wait inside if you can. Pre-arrange your Uber pickup point BEFORE entering.
Strategy 1 — Metro back
Walk to Exposición station (reverse of arrival). 10–12 min walk, then Line 1 to central Monterrey. Platform will be crowded — wait for second or third train. Trains run until midnight on match days.
Strategy 2 — Walk then Uber
Walk 10–15 minutes away from the stadium before opening Uber. Head toward Av. Pablo Livas or Blvd. Díaz Ordaz. Surge pricing drops significantly once you're away from the immediate stadium zone.
Strategy 3 — Pre-book private transport
Most San Pedro Garza García hotels offer match-day transport packages. Book when you check in. Price: ~$400–800 MXN round trip. Driver waits for you regardless of final score time.

EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS
IF I'VE BEEN ROBBED
▶
1
Do NOT resist — hand over belongings. Your life is worth more than your phone.
2
Move to a safe, public location immediately.
3
Call 911 or 078 (tourist assistance, English available).
4
File a police report (denuncia) — you need this for insurance claims. Ask for it specifically.
5
Cancel cards immediately — use numbers in this tab.
6
Contact your embassy if passport was stolen.
IF POLICE STOP ME
▶
1
Stay calm. Don't argue. Don't reach for anything without asking first.
2
Ask to call your embassy. This is your right.
3
Ask for the officer's name and badge number. Write it down.
4
If asked for a payment, calmly ask for a formal receipt (multa oficial). Corrupt officers avoid paper trails.
Tourist Police (Policía Turística) are deployed in San Pedro, Barrio Antiguo, and Macroplaza for the World Cup. They speak English and are there to help you.
IF I HAVE HEAT EXHAUSTION
▶
1
Move to shade or air conditioning immediately.
2
Drink cold water or Electrolit slowly.
3
Apply cool water to neck, wrists, and armpits.
4
Lie down with feet slightly elevated.
5
If no improvement in 15 minutes — call 911.
6
Do NOT return to the sun that day.
HEAT STROKE = MEDICAL EMERGENCY: confusion, hot dry skin (not sweating), rapid strong pulse → Call 911 immediately. Do not wait.

▶
1
