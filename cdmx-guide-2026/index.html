⚠️ Altitude Warning
You are landing at 2,240m (7,350ft). Tap to prepare.
48-Hour Survival Plan
Higher than Denver, Colorado. Your body will feel it.

Day 1: 3+ liters of water. Zero alcohol. Move slowly.
Symptoms: Headache, fatigue, dizziness, shortness of breath.
At pharmacy (Farmacias del Ahorro): Ask for "Algo para el mal de altura" or get paracetamol for headache.
When to see a doctor: Severe headache, vomiting, confusion, or chest pain. Call Hospital ABC: +52 55 5230-8000.

Most people feel normal within 24-48 hours. Stay hydrated and rest.
THE 5 RULES
1
NEVER take a street taxi
Express kidnappings are real. Uber and DiDi only, always verify the car before getting in.
2
NEVER drink tap water
Bottled water only. Look for: Bonafont, Ciel, or E-Pura. Ask for "agua embotellada" everywhere.
3
KNOW your safe neighborhoods
Polanco, Roma Norte, Condesa, Coyoacán. These are your home zones. Stay in them after dark.
4
KEEP your phone in your bag
Phone snatch-and-grab from motorcycles is the #1 tourist crime. Pocket it when walking.
5
DRINK water on Day 1
3 liters minimum. No alcohol first 24 hours. The altitude will dehydrate you faster than you expect.

NEIGHBORHOOD SAFETY ZONES
Safe day & night
Caution / day only
Avoid entirely
Polanco
Embassy district · Safest area
Mexico City's safest district. Embassy district with private security on every corner. Safe day and night including walking alone. Luxury hotels and restaurants. Paseo de la Reforma runs through it.

Watch for: Petty theft in very crowded areas only.
Getting there: Metro Polanco (Line 7, Orange) or Uber.
Roma Norte
Most popular for visitors · Walkable
The most popular neighborhood for international visitors. Walkable, vibrant, full of cafés and restaurants. Tree-lined streets, well-lit at night. Safe day and night — best walking neighborhood in CDMX.

Watch for: Phone snatching near busy traffic.
Getting there: Metro Insurgentes (Line 1, Pink).
Condesa
Parks · Residential · Adjacent to Roma
Adjacent to Roma Norte. More residential feel, parks, tree canopy. Safe day and night.

Watch for: Same as Roma Norte.
Getting there: Metro Chilpancingo (Line 9) or walk from Roma Norte (15 min).
Coyoacán
Colonial · Closest safe area to Azteca
Historic colonial neighborhood. Closest safe area to Estadio Azteca. Safe during the day.

Watch for: Walking alone after 10pm.
Best for: Day trips, cultural tourism, Frida Kahlo Museum.
Centro Histórico
Historic center · Day only
Historic center. Beautiful and worth visiting during the day. Be cautious at night. Main streets are fine. Avoid empty side streets after dark.

Watch for: Pickpockets in crowded areas.
Leave by 9pm.
Zona Rosa
Nightlife · LGBTQ+ friendly
International area between Condesa and Centro. Active nightlife, LGBTQ+ friendly.

Watch for: More crowded = higher petty crime risk.
Safe with company, less ideal alone after midnight.
Tepito
Do NOT visit
Do not visit. High crime area, no tourist reason to go here. Known for informal black markets.
Iztapalapa (residential)
Stadium area — do not explore
Where Estadio Azteca is located, but the surrounding residential areas are not safe for tourists. Go to the stadium via Metro/Tren Ligero and leave immediately after the match. Do not explore on foot.
Doctores
Pass through only during day
Transitional area south of Centro. Do not visit at night. Pass through only during day.

AFTER THE FINAL WHISTLE
⏱️ The 30-Minute Rule
Do NOT rush out at the final whistle. 80,000 people leave at once. Wait 15-20 minutes inside the stadium. Let the initial rush pass — then exit in the second wave. Much calmer.
1
Tren Ligero (Recommended)
Walk to Estadio Azteca Tren Ligero station. Trains run until midnight on match days. Crowded platform for 20-30 min then clear. Most reliable option — recommended.
2
Walk + Uber
Walk 10+ minutes from stadium before opening the app. Head toward Calzada de Tlalpan or Insurgentes Sur. Wait time reduces significantly the farther you are from the stadium.
3
Pre-arranged Hotel Shuttle
Many hotels offer match-day shuttle packages. Ask your hotel on check-in. Usually $300-600 MXN round trip. Book the day before — they fill up fast.
ARRIVING AT AICM AIRPORT
⚠️ READ THIS BEFORE YOU LAND
Men WILL approach you in the arrivals hall offering taxis. They are friendly. Some speak English. They look official. DO NOT GET IN THEIR TAXI. Express kidnappings are a documented risk. Use Uber pickup zones or official Taxi kiosks only.
Safe Airport Exit Protocol
Follow these steps every time
1
Walk past anyone offering transport
Smile and keep walking. Do not stop or engage.
2
Connect to free airport WiFi
AICM offers free WiFi throughout terminals.
3
Open Uber — request your ride
T1 pickup: Exit Door 1, rideshare area
T2 pickup: Level 1, Zone E
4
Match car + plate before getting in
Verify make, color, and license plate on app before opening the door.
5
Alternative: Official Taxi Kiosks
"Taxis Autorizados" or "TAPO" signs inside terminal. Pay fixed price in advance at kiosk.

WHAT TO DO IF...
🥷
I've been robbed
  1. Do not resist. Give them what they ask for.
  2. Walk/run opposite direction. Enter any business. Say: "Me robaron" (I was robbed).
  3. Cancel your cards immediately (numbers above).
  4. Call 911 and request a police report.
  5. File formal report at Ministerio Público. Your hotel can help you find the nearest one.
  6. Call your embassy if passport was taken.
👮
Police stopped me
Stay completely calm. No sudden movements.

Say: "¿Habla inglés?" (Do you speak English?)
Say: "Soy turista. Vine a ver el partido."

If asked for money: "Prefiero ir a la delegación."
This phrase usually ends the situation.

Do not argue. Do not film without permission.
😵
I feel sick (altitude)
1. Drink water immediately — at least 500ml.
2. Lie down if dizzy.
3. Go to any Farmacias del Ahorro.
4. Ask for: "Algo para el mal de altura"
5. Or: paracetamol for headache.

Call Hospital ABC if severe: +52 55 5230-8000
🛂
I lost my passport
  1. File police report (denuncia) — call 911.
  2. Contact your embassy (numbers above).
  3. Bring to embassy: police report, any ID, flight booking, passport photo.
  4. Emergency passport issued in 24-48 hours.

Prevention: Photograph your passport NOW and email it to yourself.
🏥
I need a doctor
Serious emergencies: Hospital ABC
+52 55 5230-8000
· Observatorio: Sur 136 No. 116, Las Américas
· Santa Fe: Av. Carlos Graef Fernández 154
English-speaking staff. Accepts international insurance.

Minor issues: Farmacias del Ahorro
Ask: "¿Hay doctor en turno?" (Is there an on-duty doctor?)
Consultation: ~$50-100 MXN
