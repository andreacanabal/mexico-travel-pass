TWO THREATS. ONE GUIDE.

Monterrey in June: 34°C (93°F) with humidity. Heat dehydrates you. Food issues dehydrate you. Together they can send you to the hospital.

RULE 1 Hydrate before you're thirsty — always.
RULE 2 Eat smart so you can enjoy the heat.
⚡ HEAT + FOOD INTERACTION
  • Digestion slows in extreme heat — eat smaller portions
  • Alcohol dehydrates TWICE as fast in the heat
  • Spicy food + heat + sun = stomach emergency
  • Your appetite may decrease — eat anyway
🟢 GREEN ZONE EAT FREELY
✅ CARNE ASADA MTY SIGNATURE ▼

Monterrey IS carne asada. Beef grilled directly on carbon (charcoal) at very high temperature — the most common food in the city. Grilled fresh to order means extremely safe.

🔥 Arrachera (skirt steak) is the local favorite. Ask: "¿Tienen arrachera?" at any asadero.
⚠️ Match day + afternoon heat + heavy carne asada meal = sluggishness. Eat carne asada after the match, not 2 hours before in the heat.
How to order
Carne asada con tortillas, por favor
✅ CABRITO ICONIC MTY ▼

Whole-roasted young goat cooked at high heat for hours. Monterrey's most iconic dish. Very safe at any established restaurant. Expensive but worth it.

📍 Find it at established restaurants in San Pedro Garza García or Barrio Antiguo.
✅ TACOS DE TROMPO SAFE ▼

Vertical spit at high heat, continuous cooking. The Monterrey version of al pastor. Safe for the same reasons: heat kills contaminants instantly.

How to order
Unos taquitos de trompo, por favor
✅ AGUA DE LIMÓN CON CHÍA BEST HEAT DRINK ▼

Lime water with chia seeds. Very popular in Monterrey. Hydrating AND has natural electrolytes from the chia. Restaurants use purified water — completely safe.

How to order
Agua de limón con chía, por favor

Caldo de res (beef broth) is perfect in Monterrey. Despite the heat, hot soup hydrates you well. Locals eat caldo year-round including summer — there's a reason.

🟡 YELLOW ZONE EAT WITH CAUTION
⚠️ STREET FOOD IN HEAT STRICTER RULES ▼

At 34°C+, the bacterial danger zone (5–60°C) is reached faster. Street food that's fine at 22°C becomes risky sooner in Monterrey's heat.

🌡️ Extra MTY rule: if it's not steaming hot OR visibly cold — don't eat it.
⚠️ MARISCOS (SEAFOOD) RESTAURANT ONLY ▼

Monterrey is not a coastal city. Any seafood has traveled from the coast. Fine at established restaurants with refrigerated supply chains.

🚫 Street mariscos in Monterrey: skip entirely.
⚠️ SPICY SAUCES IN HEAT MANAGE WISELY ▼

Your body's cooling system is already maxed. Very spicy food makes you sweat more — accelerating dehydration that's already happening from the heat.

❄️ Enjoy spicy food in air-conditioned restaurants, not at midday in direct sun.
⚠️ BEER AT THE MATCH KNOW THE MATH ▼

Beer dehydrates. Heat dehydrates. Together they work fast. Cuauhtémoc Moctezuma brewery is local to Monterrey — the beer is excellent. Just manage it.

💧 Rule: one glass of water for every beer. Non-negotiable in Monterrey heat.
🔴 RED ZONE AVOID IN MTY
🚫 FOOD IN DIRECT SUN HIGH RISK ▼

In 34°C heat, bacteria double every 20 minutes in the danger temperature range. Food on a cart in direct Monterrey sun: skip it regardless of how good it looks.

🚫 DRINKING ON EMPTY STOMACH MEDICAL EMERGENCY ▼

Heat + alcohol + empty stomach in Monterrey is the fastest path to a medical emergency. Always eat a full meal before drinking at Barrio Antiguo.

🚫 TAP WATER BOTTLED ONLY ▼

Same rule as all of Mexico. Bottled only. In Monterrey's heat, you need 3–4L per day. Bottled water is cheap and available everywhere.

🛒 Buy a large bottle at every OXXO you pass. Set a timer to drink every 30 minutes during outdoor activities.

🏪 Farmacias del Ahorro and Farmacias Benavides are the main chains. Both are everywhere in San Pedro and Barrio Antiguo. Both have doctors on duty (~$50–100 MXN consultation).

📦 BUY BEFORE MATCH DAY
  • Electrolit (3–4 bottles minimum)
  • Loperamida (diarrhea — just in case)
  • Metoclopramida (nausea)
  • Protector solar SPF 50 (sunburn accelerates dehydration)
  • 1–2L of cold water per person

🔥 HEAT STROKE — CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY IF:
  • Hot dry skin (stopped sweating in the heat)
  • Confusion or disorientation
  • Rapid, strong pulse
  • Loss of consciousness
📞 CALL 911 — DO NOT WAIT
⚡ THE WORST CASE: BOTH AT ONCE

Food poisoning AND heat exhaustion at the same time. More common than you think in Monterrey. Symptoms overlap: nausea, weakness, vomiting, dizziness.

🌡️ HEAT EXHAUSTION
  • Started during/after sun exposure
  • Heavy sweating or NO sweating
  • No vomiting/diarrhea
🦠 FOOD POISONING
  • Started 4–12 hrs after eating
  • Vomiting + diarrhea together
  • Possible fever
IF UNSURE: treat for BOTH simultaneously. Cool environment + Suero Oral + rest. If not improving in 30 minutes: call 911.
❄️ COOLING PROTOCOL
  • 1 Get to air conditioning immediately. Any hotel lobby, mall, or restaurant will work. Necesito aire acondicionado — me siento mal
  • 2 Apply cool water on neck, wrists, and forehead. Keep it there.
  • 3 Drink Electrolit or cold water immediately. Small sips if nauseous.
  • 4 Lie down with feet slightly elevated. Rest for at least 30 minutes.
  • 5 No more direct sun that day. Match or no match — your health is the priority.
